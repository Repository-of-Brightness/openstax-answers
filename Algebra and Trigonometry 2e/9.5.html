Questions 4 to 12: Find all solutions on the interval \(0 \le \theta \lt 2\pi\)

4. \(2\sin \theta = -\sqrt2\)

\(2\sin \theta = -\sqrt2\)

Divide both sides by 2 to get sine alone; isolate sine.

\(\sin \theta = -{\sqrt2 \over 2}\)

Since this is part of the Unit Circle, we can look at the unit circle for the answers when \(\theta = -{\sqrt2 \over 2}\). \(\theta\) refers to an angle.

Or if we don't want to look at the Unit Circle, we can multiply both sides by \(\sin^{-1}\) to get rid fo the sine on the left side. That way we can find the angles for \(\theta\).

Regardless, the answers would be \({3\pi}\over 4\) and \({5\pi}\over 4\)
